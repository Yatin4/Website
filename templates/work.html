Stair-Trek

Stair-Trek is a semi-automated household goods carrier capable of climbing stairs that is loadable into a vehicle's trunk with ease. Weakening of the muscles and bones is a common problem with ageing. This makes the elderly heavily dependent on others for tasks such as carrying goods, for instance, from shopping or transporting certain things from one place to another. We aim to make grocery shopping and stair-climbing a better experience for the elderly. The proposed solution is a stair-climbing grocery carrier that can be easily loaded in and out of the trunk of a vehicle, and an individual can own this product. The stair-climbing is made possible with a uniquely designed system of tracks that uses friction as the driving factor. The caster wheels are activated when traversing through flat terrains for better navigability. Various mechanisms like stair-climbing, switching (between wheels and tracks), and loading (in and out of vehicle trunk) have been developed and integrated into a single product. The user can activate each system using a control panel that is positioned near the handlebars. It has buttons to actuate the respective motor for each of the three major systems. The design was created using SOLIDWORKS and the static structural simulations were run on ANSYS Workbench. The multibody dynamic simulation was run on SOLIDWORKS too. The product reduces the risk of sudden injuries to the user and prevents any development of muscular ailments due to the activity of carrying goods up staircases. The simplified operation allows for greater independence of the user and subsequently boosts their morale in life.
