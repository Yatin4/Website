Statio

It is challenging for manual wheelchair users to apply force to lock both the wheels and release them to move. Apart from this, the existing locking mechanisms wear out over time and do not work effectively. We aim to target these issues by designing a mechanism to overcome these problems efficiently, thereby making it easier for the users to lock their wheelchairs in place whenever they feel the need to do so for performing functional activities that require the wheelchair to be in a standstill position. The proposed solution is an electro-mechanical locking mechanism that requires minimal effort for actuation, and it is retrofittable onto most wheelchairs. The design was created using SOLIDWORKS and the static structural simulations were run on ANSYS Workbench. The working mechanism's motion study was run on SOLIDWORKS too. The motor-leadscrew coupling provides the necessary power to drive the pawls into the ratchet to lock it from rotation. The electrical actuation system incorporated makes use of safely designed circuits to prevent malfunctioning errors. The product makes it much easier for paraplegic and quadriplegic patients to lock their wheelchairs. Moreover, if they feel the need to lock and unlock their wheelchairs frequently at any point in time, constantly having to apply the required force to engage and disengage the locks is difficult for the user. Statio eliminates these problems and makes the user comfortable.
